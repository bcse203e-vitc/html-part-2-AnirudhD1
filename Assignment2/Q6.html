Classic Margherita Pizza

Ingredients

  • 1 pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce (preferably fresh or passata)
  • 200g fresh mozzarella cheese, torn into pieces
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1-2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Optional: Red pepper flakes for a spicy kick

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 250°C (475°F) with a pizza stone or baking sheet inside to heat up.
  2. If you're using store-bought dough, follow the instructions for stretching. If you're making homemade dough, stretch it to a thin, round shape (about 12 inches in diameter).
  3. Place the stretched pizza dough on a parchment paper or a well-floured surface.
  4. Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the pizza dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  5. Scatter the torn mozzarella pieces over the sauce, ensuring even coverage.
  6. Drizzle a little olive oil over the top of the pizza, and sprinkle with a pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  7. Transfer the pizza to the preheated oven (on the pizza stone or baking sheet) and bake for 7-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling and slightly browned.
  8. Once out of the oven, immediately scatter the fresh basil leaves over the hot pizza.
  9. Optionally, drizzle a bit more olive oil and sprinkle red pepper flakes for extra flavor.
  10. Slice, serve, and enjoy your delicious homemade Margherita pizza!

Nutritional Information

This pizza provides a balanced mix of carbohydrates from the dough, protein and calcium from the mozzarella, and healthy fats from the olive oil. Fresh basil adds a burst of antioxidants!

Fun Fact

Did you know that the Margherita pizza was created in 1889 in honor of Queen Margherita of Savoy? The colors of the pizza (red, white, and green) are meant to represent the Italian flag!
